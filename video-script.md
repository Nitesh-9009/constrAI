# Kayakalp — 2-Minute Video Script (spoken)

Speak naturally, ~2 min total. Advance the slide at each **[Slide N]** cue.
Bracketed italics are stage directions, not spoken.

---

**[Slide 1 — Title]** *(0:00–0:12)*

Hi, we're Team [name], and this is **Kayakalp** — a predictive material control tower
for construction. It answers the one question every project keeps asking:
*"Will the material be here when the crew needs it — and if it won't, what do we do
right now?"*

**[Slide 2 — The problem]** *(0:12–0:30)*

On every job, the moment materials are ordered, the chaos begins. Five questions get
asked every single day — what's approved, what's being fabricated, what's delayed,
where is it, and will it arrive on time. The data exists, but it's scattered across
POs, emails, submittals, WhatsApp photos, and the schedule. So delays get discovered
*after* the crew is already standing idle.

**[Slide 3 — Why it stays unsolved]** *(0:30–0:42)*

Nobody has fixed this because no tool *fuses* those signals, and the ones that try
demand data entry crews will never do. Status is always reactive and point-in-time —
never predictive.

**[Slide 4 — The solution]** *(0:42–0:55)*

Kayakalp changes that. We ingest all of it, fuse it into a live material knowledge
graph, **predict** each material's arrival, **simulate** the schedule impact, and then
**act** to prevent the delay — all on one screen, ranked by cost of delay.

**[Slide 5 — How it works]** *(0:55–1:18)*

Here's the magic in three beats. You ask, *"What's blocking next week's pour?"*
Kayakalp answers: *"Rebar PO 4471 is sixty percent fabricated — seventy-eight percent
chance it lands July 14, but you need it the 11th. High risk, a four-day critical-path
slip."* Then it offers: *"Draft the supplier escalation and move the pour three days?"*
— one click, done. It doesn't just report. It acts.

**[Slide 6 — Under the hood]** *(1:18–1:33)*

This goes far deeper than an API call. A vision-language model reads job-site photos
and flags spec mismatches with no forms. Document intelligence retrieves over the
*pixels* of a drawing. A probabilistic forecaster gives calibrated arrival windows, and
an agent drafts, sends, and re-sequences.

**[Slide 7 — The technique nobody else applies]** *(1:33–1:45)*

Our edge is three research-backed moats: visual-document retrieval over drawings,
conformal-calibrated delivery predictions, and delay-cascade propagation across the
schedule graph — so we predict the domino effect, priced in dollars.

**[Slide 8 — Easy to adopt]** *(1:45–1:52)*

And it's effortless to adopt — ingest by forwarding an inbox, update by snapping a
photo, ask in plain language. It meets crews where they already work.

**[Slide 9 — Architecture]** *(1:52–1:58)*

It's all open-source and can run on-prem, so the contractor's data stays theirs.

**[Slide 10 — Impact & ask]** *(1:58–2:05)*

The result: fewer idle crews, earlier warnings, protected milestones, lower cost.
**Kayakalp — material chaos, rejuvenated into a predictive timeline.** Thank you.
